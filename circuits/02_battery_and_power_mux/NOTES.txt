02 — POWER MODULE (BUY THIS — DO NOT DESIGN)
============================================

FINAL SOLUTION
--------------
Use a ready-made module. Do not design a charger/boost circuit.

Buy one of these two:


A) BEST SIMPLE PICK — Seeed Lipo Rider Plus
   https://wiki.seeedstudio.com/Lipo-Rider-Plus/
   Also sold as: Seeed Studio "Lipo Rider Plus (Charger/Booster)"

   What you get on one board (already built):
   - USB-C input to charge the LiPo
   - Regulated 5 V output (boost, up to ~2.4 A)
   - Regulated 3.3 V output (~250 mA)
   - Battery connector / pads
   - Charge / battery LEDs

   Wire into the simulator:
   - Module 5V  → SYS_5V → P1 current limit → RJ12 pin 1
   - Module 3.3V → ESP32 VCC
   - Module GND → board GND
   - Plug 1S LiPo into the module


B) ALTERNATIVE — Adafruit bq25185 USB-C + 5V Boost
   https://www.adafruit.com/product/6106

   What you get:
   - USB-C charge in
   - Regulated 5 V out from onboard boost
   - LiPo JST

   Missing: 3.3 V → add any small 3.3 V regulator from the 5 V rail
   (circuit 03), or a ready 5V→3.3V module.


WHAT TO BUY WITH IT
-------------------
- 1S LiPo ≥ 1000 mAh, protected pack, check JST polarity
- That is enough for power. ESP32 flashing still uses the board USB-C
  (circuit 01) for data.


TEST
----
Battery only: 5 V and 3.3 V present.
USB-C on the power module: battery charges; 5 V stays regulated.
Unplug USB-C: rails stay up.
