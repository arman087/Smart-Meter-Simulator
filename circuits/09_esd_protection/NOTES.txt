09 — ESD PROTECTION (USB + RJ12)
================================

WHAT THIS BLOCK IS
------------------
Lab cables get plugged a lot. ESD kills USB PHYs and MOSFET gates.
Cheap insurance: TVS / ESD arrays on exposed connectors.

NECESSARY PIECES
----------------
1. USB: dedicated ESD on D+ / D- (and optionally VBUS).
2. RJ12: clamp pins 1, 2, 5 (power + signals). Pins 3/6 are GND.
3. Short path from TVS to connector GND pad (low inductance).

RECOMMENDED PARTS
-----------------
USB:
  - USBLC6-2SC6 (used on P1 Ghost) — perfect reuse
  - or TPD2E2U06 / PESD5V0x1UL

RJ12 / general:
  - PESD5V0L2BT / PESD5V0S1UL on P1_REQ and P1_DATA
  - SMAJ5.0A or similar on P1_5V if you want beefier power clamp
  - Multi-channel ESD arrays (e.g. TPD4E05U06) if you like one chip

Modules: not really needed — discrete TVS are tiny and cheap.
If you want zero thinking: "USB ESD protector breakout" for USB only.

HUMAN / SIMILAR REFERENCES
--------------------------
- IEC 61000-4-2 mentions in DSMR P1 safety section (context).
- TI / ST ESD app notes for USB 2.0 high-speed routing.
- P1 Ghost schematic: USBLC6 next to USB-C.

KICAD
-----
Place ESD within a few mm of the connector. Via to GND plane under TVS.

TEST
----
No easy home test for ESD rating. Continuity: TVS not shorted;
signal edges still clean at 115200 on Data.
