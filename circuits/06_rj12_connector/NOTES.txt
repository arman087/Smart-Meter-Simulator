06 — RJ12 CONNECTOR (P1 METER JACK)
===================================

WHAT THIS BLOCK IS
------------------
Physical DSMR P1 port. On the METER / simulator you use a FEMALE 6P6C
(RJ12) jack. OSM (Ghost) plugs in with a male RJ12 cable.

PINOUT (DSMR) — meter side
--------------------------
 1  +5V         SOURCE from Lipo Rider 5V (SYS_5V / P1_5V)
 2  Data Request  INPUT sense (circuit 07)
 3  Data GND    → system GND
 4  n.c.        leave open
 5  Data        open-collector OUT (circuit 08)
 6  Power GND   → system GND

NECESSARY PIECES
----------------
1. RJ12 / 6P6C PCB jack (same family as Ghost is ideal for cable compatibility).
2. Silk labels 1…6 matching the standard (check datasheet pin 1 orientation —
   easy to mirror!).
3. Short fat traces for pins 1 and 6; star GND to digital section if noisy.
4. Optional: shroud / panel cutout note for enclosure.

RECOMMENDED PARTS
-----------------
- KH-PCB-6P6C or equivalent 6P6C vertical / right-angle (Ghost used KH-PCB-6P6C)
- Ready breakout for bench: TinyTronics "RJ12 to Dupont" adapters
  https://www.tinytronics.nl (search RJ12)

KICAD
-----
Reuse Ghost RJ12 footprint from EasyEDA import if pin numbers match
the datasheet. Double-check pin 1 with continuity on a scrap jack.

TEST
----
Continuity: PCB net P1_5V ↔ jack pin 1, etc.
Cable into Ghost: Ghost sees 5 V on its pin 1 when simulator powered.
